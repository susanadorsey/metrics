Top 6 Recommendations

  1. Improve AI Bot CSAT & Expand Scope
    AI Bot CSAT dropped from 3.0 in April to 2.7 in May, even as handled volume increased to 276 closed tickets. That higher May volume may be part of why more training is needed before expanding scope further.
  2. Reduce WISMO at the Source
    WISMO still accounts for 190 May tickets. Better order-status visibility, clearer lead times, and tighter vendor ETA communication should remove one of the largest repeat-contact drivers in the queue.
  3. Convert Pre-Sale Tickets into Revenue
    Pre-sale tickets remain a meaningful revenue opportunity. Better product pages, FAQs, install guides, and stock visibility should reduce friction while improving conversion.
  4. Continue to Close the Ticket Fields Gap
    Several Ticket Fields were added since the April report, and that progress should continue. “Other Vendor” and broad product buckets are still large enough to limit reporting precision and decision quality.
  5. Vendor Accountability Scorecard
    A relatively small set of vendors still drives a disproportionate share of support burden. Use that list to prioritize supplier conversations, merchandising cleanup, and expectation-setting improvements.
  6. Phone Channel Decision
    Dropped calls remain material. Evaluate whether tighter deflection, callback support, or a clearer no-phone policy best matches the business goal.
